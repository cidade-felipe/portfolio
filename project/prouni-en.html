Prouni Data Insights

Visual analysis with public PROUNI data to understand beneficiary profile, historical evolution and state-level distribution through an interactive map.

Author: Felipe Cidade Soares Type: EDA and Visualization

Summary

1. Introduction 2. Analysis questions 3. Source and preparation 3.1 Pipeline 4. Visual analysis and insights 5. Conclusion 6. Next steps

1. Introduction

This project explores public PROUNI data with focus on visualization and interpretation. The goal is to transform a large dataset into clear answers: who receives scholarships, how it changes over time, and how it is distributed across Brazil.

2. Analysis questions

Some questions that guide the project: what is the proportion of full vs. partial scholarships, how demographic profile is distributed (age and gender), how it evolves over the years, and which states concentrate more grants.

3. Source and preparation

The dataset goes through cleaning, standardization and structuring for analysis. The objective is to reduce noise, ensure consistency, and prepare data for charts and state-level maps. This process is essential to ensure visualizations reflect data reality and generate reliable insights.

The pipeline includes reading, null handling, column renaming, age filtering and creation of new variables, plus specific adjustments for visualization. 4. Visual analysis and insights

The project focuses on Plotly visualizations, including year-by-year comparisons, gender and age cuts, and an interactive GeoJSON map to show state-level distribution. The analysis revealed consistent patterns in PROUNI scholarship distribution over time, indicating relevant regional, demographic and socioeconomic differences.

4.1. Uneven regional expansion

North and Northeast regions showed the highest percentage growth over the years, especially after 2013, indicating interiorization and broader access to higher education outside traditional major urban centers.

Even so, the Southeast remains the main absolute concentration of scholarships, showing that despite growth in other regions, regional inequality is still significant.

4.2. Concentration by racial profile

Data shows that self-declared mixed-race (pardo) and white students account for most scholarships granted in the analyzed period. Growth in these groups is consistent over the years.

In contrast, participation of Indigenous students remains low with limited growth, indicating a possible access bottleneck for this specific group.

4.3. Gender differences

A faster increase in female participation is observed over time, especially for full scholarships. From 2014 onward, women represent the largest share of beneficiaries.

This pattern suggests stronger retention and engagement of women in access-to-higher-education programs through public policy.

4.4. Predominance of full scholarships

Most grants are full scholarships, especially among higher-population groups. This reinforces PROUNI's role as an effective educational inclusion mechanism for students in socioeconomic vulnerability.

Partial scholarships show lower volume and more moderate growth, suggesting that the program mainly prioritizes students with lower ability to afford tuition.

4.5. Peak and stabilization after 2015

In nearly all analyzed cuts, there is a peak between 2014 and 2016, followed by stabilization or slight decline.

This behavior may be associated with economic changes, public policy adjustments, and budget restrictions in the post-crisis period.

5. Conclusion

Analysis of PROUNI data from 2005 to 2019 highlights a relevant expansion process of access to higher education in Brazil, with significant growth in total scholarships over the period.

A substantial increase is observed in North and Northeast regions, although the Southeast still concentrates the highest absolute number of grants. This behavior reveals progress in interiorizing access, but also reinforces persistent structural regional inequalities.

From a demographic perspective, rising female participation and consistent growth of historically underrepresented groups indicate positive impact of inclusion public policies. Even so, low representation of certain groups suggests equity challenges remain.

Overall, results show that PROUNI plays an important role in democratizing higher education, especially through predominance of full scholarships. At the same time, data reinforces the importance of continuous monitoring and evidence-based analysis to guide policy adjustments and improvements.

This project demonstrates how public data, when organized and interpreted rigorously, can generate concrete insights about social impact and distribution of educational opportunities.

6. Next steps

To deepen the analysis, it would be useful to include more recent PROUNI data, especially to evaluate pandemic impacts and post-2019 trends. In addition, crossing data with ENEM and School Census information could enrich understanding of beneficiary profiles and educational trajectories.

Another possibility is to explore predictive analyses to identify factors that influence scholarship granting and beneficiary academic performance using machine learning techniques.

Finally, developing an interactive dashboard with key indicators and visualizations could facilitate access to these insights for public managers, researchers and society in general.
